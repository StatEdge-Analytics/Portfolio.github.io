Problem Definition

The business requires a clear and structured way to monitor biscuit sales performance across multiple cities, time periods, and customer segments. Revenue, expenses, and profit data exist but are not easily interpretable when spread across raw transaction records. There is a need to understand how different locations, payment methods, and customer demographics contribute to overall performance.

Core Objectives

  • Implement a demand-driven revenue optimization model.
  • Optimize product portfolio efficiency.
  • Balance regional growth and replicate successful market strategies.

Present-State Insight

Revenue shows volatility across months and days. Top SKUs dominate sales contribution, creating concentration risk. Regional analysis reveals uneven market performance, while weekly trends show untapped potential.

Report Preview

Future-State Strategy

Deploy predictive demand forecasting to reduce volatility. Introduce dynamic pricing and portfolio optimization. Replicate successful city strategies and align marketing with data-driven timing.

Decision Framework Mapping

  • Pricing, inventory, and marketing decisions are informed by demand patterns.
  • Regional and product insights guide expansion and resource allocation.
  • Strategy ensures all actions are measurable and data-backed.

Data Credibility

Transactional data is reliable for descriptive analysis. Limitations include missing external factors (competition, seasonality drivers). Top-product focus may overshadow smaller opportunities.
